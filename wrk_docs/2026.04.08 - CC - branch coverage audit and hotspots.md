## What This Means

There are three distinct coverage problems here:

1. `src\silk\encoder.rs` and `src\opus\encoder.rs` dominate the remaining debt. They are large, stateful, and branch-heavy.
2. `src\dnn\lpcnet.rs` and `src\opus\multistream.rs` are still attractive because they have large missed-line counts with more deterministic setup.
3. `src\opus\decoder.rs` and `src\opus\repacketizer.rs` are branch-cleanup targets that can likely move faster than the big encoders.

The branch metric is the bottleneck. Pushing line coverage alone will not get the repo honestly close to 98%.

## Uncovered-Line Pattern Notes

The missing-line report shows a few useful patterns:

- `dnn::lpcnet` still has several dense untouched regions rather than only isolated tail lines.
- `opus::multistream` has long cold blocks in packet/layout/control handling, which is usually good unit-test territory.
- `opus::decoder` and `opus::repacketizer` still have branch-heavy packet-shape and wrapper error paths left.
- `silk::encoder` remains a very large untouched surface. It is still the single biggest lever, but also the highest setup cost.

## Practical Conclusion

1. There are no failing tests blocking branch-aware coverage in the current checkout.
2. The immediate work should target deterministic, medium-cost hotspots first: `dnn::lpcnet`, `opus::multistream`, `opus::decoder`, and `opus::repacketizer`.
3. Hitting 98% across all reported metrics will eventually require sustained work in `silk::encoder` and `opus::encoder`. There is no honest shortcut around that.
