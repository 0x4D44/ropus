# Journal - DRED/DNN Parity Review

Date: 2026.05.06

## Review Start

I took the independent reviewer role for Ohm's DRED/DNN coverage-contract implementation. I read the HLD and signoff first, then inspected the target diff only. I kept the scope tight because the important failure mode here is contract drift, not broad codec behavior.

Initial emotional temperature: focused and cautious. This is exactly the kind of testing infrastructure change where a small wording mismatch can make a green report overclaim coverage. I wanted to verify the policy source of truth before looking at cosmetic reporting.

## What I Checked

The implementation introduces a `PreflightPolicy` enum and derives it from `quick` plus `release_preflight`. That matches the HLD requirement to stop passing only a boolean into preflight. The same derived policy is also used to select the quick Stage 2 profile, so quick release-preflight and no-neural-claim behavior stay aligned.

The asset policy is tempdir-tested: default/no release leaves missing neural assets optional/report-only; quick release-preflight also leaves them optional and says the neural/DRED gates are not claimed; non-quick release-preflight makes `dnn_base_weights`, `dred_rdovae_weights`, and `float_deep_plc_assets` required and banner-blocking when missing.

The reporting surface now exposes the profile and claim in JSON and HTML, and the CLI help no longer describes release-preflight as a core-only profile. The oracle matrix keeps exact DRED payload-byte parity and DRED PCM reconstruction as explicit debt.

I checked the diff names for forbidden scope changes and found only full-test files plus the oracle matrix and implementation journal.

## Outcome

I found no blocking issues and no required feedback. Residual risk remains exactly where the HLD says it should remain: this slice makes claims honest, but it does not solve byte-exact DRED payload parity or DRED PCM reconstruction.

Emotional temperature at close: steady. The change is narrow, the tests are pointed at the policy contract, and the wording is blunt enough that a future reader should not mistake quick release-preflight for neural/DRED coverage.
