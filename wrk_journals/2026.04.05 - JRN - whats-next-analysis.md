## Recommended Priority

### 1. Tier A near-misses (highest ROI: up to 11 tests, minimal effort)
The one-byte-off tests at 128kbps suggest a single accumulated rounding error. Strategy:
- Add a per-frame byte comparison that stops at the first diverging frame
- Identify which frame diverges first (frame 40+, likely near the end)
- Add debug prints for that specific frame's encoding steps

### 2. Tier B stereo at lower sample rates (9 tests, moderate effort)
These only fail at 8k/12k/16k/24k, not 48kHz. What's different?
- Lower sample rates use shorter MDCT sizes (fewer bins per band)
- The bandwidth decision might differ (narrowband vs wideband cutoff)
- Resampling is involved for non-48kHz input
- The Opus encoder's bandwidth selection was flagged as buggy (interpolation formula wrong)
Could fixing the bandwidth selection resolve these?

### 3. Tier C SILK (20 tests, high effort, deep investigation)
The rate control loop needs per-iteration tracing to find where C and Rust diverge. This is the deepest bug and will take the most effort. Defer until Tiers A and B are resolved.

### 4. Tier D sine1k (8 tests, unknown effort)
Needs targeted investigation. Could be PVQ-related, could be a prefilter interaction with short pitch periods. Lower priority since these are specialized test signals.
