## 02: Assessment

The prior campaign has done the right kind of work: hidden false-greens are now
mostly named. Harness mismatches fail, fuzz target discovery is current, weak
fuzz oracles are labeled, PLC drift is an active bounded oracle, DRED/DNN claims
require assets in non-quick release preflight, corpus claims are manifest-backed,
and performance has release-threshold semantics.

The remaining candidates split into two categories:

- Testing-infrastructure gaps: platform/sanitizer breadth, mutation test
  revival, corpus breadth.
- Codec/product debts: DRED exact payload/PCM, PLC full-frame exact parity,
  performance regression investigation.

My recommendation is platform/sanitizer breadth next. It is the cleanest
remaining infrastructure slice and complements the current optimized
`x86-64-v3` path. Mutation testing is useful but slower and interpretive. DRED,
PLC, and performance follow-up are important, but each can turn into
implementation/debugging rather than test-gate hardening.

Feeling: this is the point where discipline matters. The project has enough
known red/yellow signals that mixing them into one "next slice" would produce a
muddy design and muddy blame when something fails.
