## Context — why this campaign exists

The first 24h campaign (closed 2026-05-02 21:36 BST) found 10 classes
including 4 real codec bugs but saturated by T+5h. The cluster spent
~16 hours flat because:
- The "real bug" workers (encode, multistream, multiframe, roundtrip)
  re-died on every restart against the same known classes.
- 12 of 22 workers were retired permanently.
- libFuzzer corpus had grown to 290 MB but was never minimised.
- A follow-up "encoder-divergence-fixes" supervisor was launched
  later on 2026-05-02 but didn't land any codec fixes — those bugs
  are still live.

This campaign is structured to **escape that saturation** without
touching the codec.

  → ROUNDTRIP-path bytes mismatch (15B vs 15B different)

Diagnosis — TWO bugs in v1 launcher:
1. `-fork=N` alone does **not** survive child crashes. With the default
   `-ignore_crashes=0` libFuzzer's parent process exits when any child
   panics. I had set =0 explicitly thinking that meant "save the crash"
   — but =0 means "treat crashes as fatal". Need =1.
2. Skip filters too narrow:
   - encode_multiframe filter checked `app == VOIP`. New crash at AUDIO
     proves the bug isn't application-gated. Drop the app constraint.
   - multistream filter only gated the encode-path assert. Same class
     fires on the roundtrip-path's bytes assert at line ~585. Apply
     to both.

Both crashes are NEW VARIANTS of EXISTING classes, not new root causes.
Saved as repros under `known_failures/`:
- `multiframe-vbr-fec-dtx-divergence/crash-audio-cx9.bin`
- `multistream-encode-bytes-divergence/crash-04-roundtrip-cx4-vbr1.bin`
