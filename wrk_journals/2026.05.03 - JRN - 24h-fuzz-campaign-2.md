### What worked

1. **`-fork=N` + `-ignore_crashes=1`** is the canonical resilience
   pattern for differential fuzzing against a target with known
   divergence classes. Campaign 1's "one bug → one dead worker"
   failure mode is gone. Without `-ignore_crashes=1`, the default
   fork mode still exits on crash — easy mistake (campaign 2 v1 hit
   this within 5 min, hence the v2 relaunch).

2. **Per-class skip filters narrow but explicit**. The filter doesn't
   need to cover every variant — it needs to cover enough that the
   PARENT process doesn't get rate-limited by capture overhead.
   Combined with ignore_crashes=1, even unfilteres variants just bump
   a counter and respawn the child.

3. **`cargo fuzz cmin` before launch**. 4.3× corpus shrink (268 MB →
   62 MB) cut replay time on every fork-child startup. Real win on
   forky targets — multistream's job count rolls every ~150-200 s,
   so reduced startup cost compounds.

4. **fuzz_decode_plc_seq as new target**. 12798 cov on its own surface
   in 20.5 h, 0 panics. The "0 panics" is itself signal — either the
   PLC sequence path is truly clean or the oracle's pragmatic
   "skip SILK/Hybrid PCM compare" carve-out catches the recovery
   class. Either way, no new bugs surfaced where the SILK
   recovery-divergence class was supposed to.

5. **Asynchronous codec fix**. Another agent's harness-modulus fix
   (`05702b5`) landed mid-campaign and the rebuilt binary auto-rotated
   into the fork children. Net +5448 cov on multistream, +4699 on
   encode within one cycle. The campaign acted as a live regression
   test for the fix.

### What didn't

1. **5-min smoke on v1 missed the launcher bug**. I assumed `-fork=N`
   was the resilience knob; turned out to need `-ignore_crashes=1`
   too. Cost: ~1 hour of campaign time + a relaunch. Lesson: smoke-test
   fork-mode resilience by deliberately injecting a panic (e.g., by
   running the launcher against a known-failing repro for 5 min) before
   trusting it for a 20-hour campaign.

2. **External edits to my fuzz target files**. Three of my filter
   edits got rolled back between Edit and the next read (I assume
   another agent or linter). Re-applied + immediately committed —
   solved by speed, but a fragile pattern.

3. **Hourly monitor went through 3 versions** before reading the
   right paths under the right CWD with robust cov extraction.
   None of the bugs cost the campaign anything material; pure
   instrumentation cleanup.
