## Insights

- The next useful performance slice is not vector expansion. More vectors would
  just create more smoke-only rows unless the profile/threshold contract is
  fixed first.
- A single 1.15x threshold is the wrong shape for release failure. It is too
  close to the current SILK WB encode baseline and not tied to measured
  variance.
- Same-run Rust/C ratios are still the best gate primitive because absolute
  times are too host-specific.
- Quick release-preflight must keep making no performance claim. Trying to make
  quick both fast and performance-meaningful would be dishonest.

## Emotional Temperature

Calm, but slightly wary of the word "threshold." Performance gates are easy to
make look rigorous while actually encoding someone's noisy desktop state. The
design needs to force measurement and claim labels up front so we do not build a
flaky release blocker with a confident name.
