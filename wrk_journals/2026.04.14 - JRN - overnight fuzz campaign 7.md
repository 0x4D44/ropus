### Investigation & Fix (2026-04-15)

**Root cause (shared by both bugs)**: `celt_fir` saturation range mismatch.

The C reference on x86 dispatches to `celt_fir_sse4_1` which uses `_mm_packs_epi32`
for i32→i16 conversion. This intrinsic saturates to **[-32768, 32767]**. The Rust
implementation used `sround16(sum, SIG_SHIFT)` which calls `saturate(x, 32767)`,
clamping to **[-32767, 32767]** — off by one at the negative extreme.

When an excitation sample hit exactly -32768 in C but was clamped to -32767 in Rust,
`celt_maxabs16` returned different values (32768 vs 32767). This caused the `shift`
parameter in the decay energy computation to differ (10 vs 8), which propagated through
the decay factor and accumulated over multiple PLC frames (Bug A) or encoder frames (Bug B).

**Fix** (`src/celt/lpc.rs`): Changed `celt_fir` output saturation from
`sround16(sum, SIG_SHIFT)` to `pshr32(sum, SIG_SHIFT).clamp(-32768, 32767)`,
matching the C SSE4.1 path. Both the unrolled 4-at-a-time loop and scalar tail
were updated.
