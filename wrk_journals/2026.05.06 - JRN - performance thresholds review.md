## 02: Findings

The profile plumbing and release-blocking semantics are mostly right.
Non-quick `--release-preflight --skip-benchmarks` fails rather than downgrading.
The gate uses per-vector/per-operation same-run Rust/C ratios, not the old
global `1.15x` WARN threshold.

I found two issues:

- The `48k_sine1k_loud.wav` live smoke measurement shows decode at `8.74x`,
  while the threshold table still gates that row at `1.26x`. This should not be
  papered over, but it needs an explicit calibration or regression decision
  before the slice is called complete.
- Missing-threshold and release-thresholded crashed-row cases are implemented
  but not covered by the synthetic unit tests, despite being required
  release-blocking classes in the signed HLD review.

Emotional temperature: the implementation is directionally good, but I would not
sign it off yet. The 8.74x measurement is exactly the kind of surprise this gate
is supposed to surface; ignoring it would undermine the whole testing effort.
